What it is

A separate legal person regulated by the Companies Act, 2013.

A Private Limited Company is the most recognised corporate form in India. It gives founders limited liability, perpetual succession, clean separation between owner and business, and the structure that every VC, bank and enterprise customer expects before they sign.

Since 2016 all incorporations go through a single web form – SPICe+ (INC-32). Part A reserves the company name through RUN; Part B handles incorporation, MoA (INC-33) and AoA (INC-34), the AGILE-PRO-S linked form for GSTIN, EPFO, ESIC, profession tax, bank account opening and shop establishment registration, and INC-9 director declarations. The entire filing is signed using Class 3 Digital Signature Certificates of the proposed directors and subscribers.

SPICe+ Part A

Name reservation with two options; 20 days to file Part B once approved.

SPICe+ Part B

Incorporation application, MoA, AoA, declarations and registered office proof.

AGILE-PRO-S

GSTIN, EPFO, ESIC, profession tax, bank account – all in one linked form.

PAN, TAN & COI

Issued by the MCA along with the Certificate of Incorporation.

Our process

Six steps to an incorporation certificate.

1

Discovery & name shortlisting

We understand your business, capital structure and shareholding, check the MCA and trademark databases and shortlist names that are likely to get cleared in the first attempt.

2

Digital Signature Certificates

We arrange Class 3 DSC for every proposed director and subscriber. DIN is generated inside SPICe+ itself for up to three first directors – no separate DIR-3.

3

SPICe+ Part A

We file Part A with two proposed names and main business objects. Once approved, the name stays reserved for 20 days to complete Part B.

4

MoA, AoA & Part B filing

We draft MoA (INC-33) and AoA (INC-34) tailored to your shareholding, capital structure and object clauses, prepare INC-9 declarations, and file Part B along with AGILE-PRO-S.

5

Certificate of Incorporation

The Registrar issues the Certificate of Incorporation along with the Corporate Identity Number (CIN), company PAN and TAN. We share digital copies and briefed next-steps on WhatsApp.

6

Bank account & post-incorporation setup

Current account, INC-20A commencement of business filing, first board meeting, statutory registers and initial compliance calendar – we hand the company over cleanly.

Frequently asked

Private Limited questions, answered.

How many directors and shareholders do I need?

add

A private limited company needs a minimum of two directors and two shareholders (they can be the same people), and at least one director must be a resident of India. The maximum is fifteen directors without a special resolution and 200 shareholders. If you are a solo founder, a One Person Company (OPC) is the alternative under the same chapter.

Is there a minimum paid-up capital?

add

The minimum paid-up capital requirement (earlier one lakh for private and five lakh for public companies) was removed by the Companies (Amendment) Act, 2015. You can start with any capital that is practical – it is shown in the MoA as authorised capital and actually brought in as paid-up capital after incorporation and the bank account.

How is a Pvt Ltd different from an LLP?

add

A Pvt Ltd has shares, board meetings, statutory audit regardless of size, and stricter annual compliance – but it is the structure that funding, ESOPs and enterprise customers expect. An LLP has partners, capital contribution instead of shares, lighter compliance and no statutory audit below prescribed thresholds, but it is not fundraising-friendly and cannot issue ESOPs. We help founders pick based on the three-year view of the business, not just the launch day.

What is the post-incorporation compliance burden?

add

Within 180 days of incorporation you must file INC-20A (commencement of business) after bringing in paid-up capital. The first board meeting must be held within 30 days. Each year you file AOC-4 (financials) and MGT-7A (annual return), hold an AGM, and conduct a statutory audit – the audit is mandatory for every Pvt Ltd regardless of turnover. DIR-3 KYC for each director is due annually. We build a compliance calendar into the handover so nothing is missed.

When and how does the bank account open?

add

The current account is now a part of the AGILE-PRO-S linked form inside SPICe+, with partner banks. Some founders still prefer to open an account directly with their bank after incorporation using the COI, PAN, MoA/AoA, KYC of directors and a board resolution. We coordinate with whichever route you choose and make sure the paid-up capital is deposited in the first 180 days.
