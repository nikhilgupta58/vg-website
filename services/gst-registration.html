What it is

A single national registration for goods and services tax.

GST registration gives your business a fifteen-digit GSTIN, which is the single identity recognised across every state under the CGST, SGST and IGST framework. It lets you collect tax on your invoices, claim input tax credit on your purchases, and apply for tenders, marketplaces and export benefits that expect a valid GSTIN.

The aggregate turnover threshold is forty lakh for exclusive supply of goods and twenty lakh for services in most states. Special-category states (including Uttarakhand, the North-East, Himachal Pradesh and Jammu & Kashmir) have a lower ten-lakh ceiling for services. Inter-state supply, e-commerce operators, casual taxable persons, non-resident taxable persons and reverse-charge taxpayers must register regardless of turnover.

Regular scheme

Full input tax credit, monthly or quarterly GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B filing.

Composition scheme

Flat rate, quarterly CMP-08 and annual GSTR-4, intended for small suppliers.

Our process

From TRN to GSTIN in six steps.

1

Document review

We check every document against the current REG-01 requirements, fix the usual red flags (name mismatch, blurry NOC, outdated electricity bill) before filing.

2

Part A of REG-01

We submit basic details (PAN, mobile, email) on the GST portal and obtain a Temporary Reference Number (TRN) after mobile and email OTP verification.

3

Part B & document upload

Using the TRN we file Part B with business details, promoter details, principal place, additional places, HSN/SAC and bank details. All documents are uploaded in the required format.

4

Aadhaar authentication & verification

We complete Aadhaar-based e-KYC of the promoter which speeds up processing. Non-Aadhaar applications now need biometric verification at a Facilitation Centre.

5

ARN & officer review

On submission you get an Application Reference Number (ARN). If the jurisdictional officer raises a clarification in REG-03, we draft and upload the reply in REG-04 within the statutory window.

6

GSTIN & certificate REG-06

Once approved, we hand over the fifteen-digit GSTIN and registration certificate REG-06, brief you on invoice format, HSN on invoices and the first return due date.

Frequently asked

GST registration questions.

Is GST registration mandatory if my turnover is below the threshold?

add

Not just on turnover – but there are compulsory triggers regardless of turnover: any inter-state taxable supply, selling through an e-commerce operator, acting as a casual or non-resident taxable person, supplies under reverse charge, agents of a supplier, input service distributors, and anyone supplying on behalf of another registered person. If any of these apply, registration is mandatory from the very first rupee.

Do I need a separate registration for each state?

add

Yes. GST is state-specific. If you have a taxable place of business in more than one state, each state needs its own registration. Within a single state, multiple verticals or branches can either be added as additional places of business on one registration or taken as separate registrations for different business verticals.

How long does GST registration take?

add

With Aadhaar authentication and clean documents, straightforward cases are usually approved within about seven working days. Cases without Aadhaar authentication, or where biometric verification is required, take longer because the applicant has to visit a GST Suvidha Kendra or Facilitation Centre.

Why do applications get rejected?

add

The usual reasons are: unclear or outdated address proof, mismatch between the NOC and the electricity bill, bank account not in the name of the business, HSN selected incorrectly, photographs that do not meet spec, or not replying to REG-03 within seven working days. We pre-empt all of these during our document review so you do not lose the first filing attempt.

Can I claim input tax credit from the day I register?

add

Yes – and on stock held on the day before the effective date of registration if you file form ITC-01 within thirty days. You can claim ITC on raw materials, semi-finished and finished goods in stock, subject to the normal restrictions under section 17. This is one of the strongest reasons to voluntarily register even before crossing the threshold if your suppliers are already charging you GST.
