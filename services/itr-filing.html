What it is

The annual return every taxpayer files with the Income Tax Department.

An Income Tax Return is the statement of income, deductions, taxes paid and any refund due that every eligible person files each year on the e-filing portal. The due date for most non-audit taxpayers is 31 July following the financial year, and 31 October for audit cases.

The department offers seven return forms, from ITR-1 Sahaj for resident salaried individuals up to ITR-7 for trusts and political parties. Picking the wrong form is one of the most common causes of defective notices under section 139(9). We handle form selection, schedule-wise data entry, regime comparison between the old and the new default regime under section 115BAC, and the full e-verification flow using Aadhaar OTP, net banking or DSC.

ITR-1 & ITR-2

Salaried individuals, one house property, capital gains, foreign assets.

ITR-3 & ITR-4

Business, profession, presumptive taxation under 44AD, 44ADA and 44AE.

ITR-5 & ITR-6

LLPs, partnership firms, AOPs and companies other than those claiming 11.

ITR-7

Trusts, charitable institutions, research associations, political parties.

Frequently asked

ITR questions we hear every July.

Do I need to file if my income is below the basic exemption limit?

add

Usually no, but there are mandatory filing triggers even below the slab: electricity bills above one lakh in a year, foreign travel spends above two lakh, deposits of one crore or more in a current account, business turnover above sixty lakh, professional gross receipts above ten lakh, TDS/TCS of twenty-five thousand or more (fifty thousand for senior citizens), and holding any foreign asset or being a signing authority on a foreign account. In all these cases filing is compulsory.

What happens if I miss the 31 July deadline?

add

You can still file a belated return under section 139(4) until 31 December of the assessment year, but a late filing fee under 234F applies (smaller if total income is below five lakh) along with interest under 234A on any unpaid tax. You also lose the ability to carry forward most business and capital losses, and you cannot switch to the old regime if you were otherwise eligible.

Can I revise my return if I discover a mistake?

add

Yes. Under section 139(5) you can file a revised return any number of times until 31 December of the assessment year or before the return is assessed, whichever is earlier. If the window is over, an updated return (ITR-U) under section 139(8A) is available for up to 48 months from the end of the assessment year, subject to paying additional tax.

New regime vs old regime – which should I pick?

add

The new regime is now the default and offers wider slabs and a higher standard deduction but strips out most chapter VI-A deductions and HRA. The old regime still wins when you have a running home loan, large 80C investments, HRA in a metro and mediclaim. We run both computations for every client so the choice is based on your real numbers, not a generic rule of thumb. Salaried taxpayers can switch each year; business and professional income has a one-time switch.

How long does the refund take to hit my bank?

add

Once the return is e-verified, CPC Bangalore usually processes straightforward returns within a few weeks and credits the refund directly to your pre-validated bank account. Returns with scrutiny flags, heavy capital gains or large TDS mismatches take longer. We track the processing status and the 143(1) intimation, and flag any adjustment immediately.
