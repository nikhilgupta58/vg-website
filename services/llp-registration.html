What it is

Partnership flexibility, company-style limited liability.

A Limited Liability Partnership is a body corporate under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008. It combines the internal flexibility of a partnership with the limited liability of a company – one partner is not personally liable for the acts or misconduct of another, and creditors can only look to the LLP's assets, not the partners' personal assets.

Incorporation is done through the FiLLiP (Form for Incorporation of Limited Liability Partnership) on the MCA portal. DPIN for designated partners, name reservation and the incorporation application all happen inside the same form. Once the LLP is incorporated, the LLP Agreement must be filed in Form 3 within 30 days – it is the internal constitution defining profit share, contribution and management rights.

Our process

Five steps from idea to LLPIN.

1

Structuring & name check

We understand the business, discuss profit share and contribution, then shortlist two LLP names and pre-check them against MCA and trademark records.

2

DSC & DPIN

We issue Class 3 DSC for each proposed designated partner. DPIN for up to two designated partners is applied inside FiLLiP itself – no separate form.

3

FiLLiP incorporation

We file the integrated FiLLiP form with name, partners, office, consent and declarations. Subscriber sheet and office proof are attached.

4

Certificate of Incorporation & LLPIN

Once approved, the Registrar issues the Certificate of Incorporation with the LLPIN and the LLP PAN/TAN. We share the digital set on WhatsApp.

5

LLP Agreement & Form 3

We draft the LLP Agreement on the correct state-stamp paper (Uttarakhand or your state), get it signed and notarised, and file Form 3 within 30 days of incorporation to avoid the daily penalty.

Frequently asked

LLP questions, straight answers.

LLP vs Private Limited – which should I choose?

add

Pick a Pvt Ltd if you plan to raise equity funding, issue ESOPs to employees, or sell to enterprises that require a company vendor. Pick an LLP if you are a service or professional firm, do not plan to dilute equity, and want lighter year-round compliance. An LLP cannot issue shares, so fund-raising usually means converting to a company later – which is possible but takes time.

How is an LLP taxed?

add

An LLP is taxed as a firm at a flat rate with surcharge and cess. Partners' remuneration and interest on capital are deductible to the LLP (within section 40(b) limits) and taxable in the hands of the partners. The share of profit received by partners is exempt. There is no concept of Dividend Distribution Tax or MAT – LLPs pay Alternate Minimum Tax (AMT) under section 115JC in certain cases.

What are the annual compliance filings?

add

Every LLP files Form 11 (Annual Return) by 30 May and Form 8 (Statement of Account and Solvency) by 30 October each year, even if there has been no business activity. The income tax return is due by 31 July, or 31 October if a tax audit applies. A statutory audit under the LLP Act is only required if turnover exceeds forty lakh or contribution exceeds twenty-five lakh. Late filings attract a daily penalty, so the calendar discipline really matters.

What is an LLP ideal for?

add

Professional services firms – law, architecture, design, consulting, marketing, engineering – are the classic use case. So are family businesses formalising profit shares, small trading ventures that do not want statutory audit, and SPVs for real-estate or joint-venture projects. Anything that benefits from limited liability but does not need to issue shares is a good fit.

Can an LLP be converted to a company (or vice versa)?

add

Yes. A private limited company can convert to an LLP under section 56 of the LLP Act if all shareholders become partners and there are no secured creditor objections. Conversion of an LLP to a private limited company is possible under section 366 of the Companies Act, 2013 with a minimum of two partners, a newspaper notice and the standard SPICe+ process. We handle both directions.
