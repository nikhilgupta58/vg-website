What it is

Official recognition by DPIIT, Government of India.

Startup India is the flagship initiative of the Government of India, administered by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT). DPIIT recognition is a formal status granted to eligible early-stage ventures that unlocks a curated set of tax and regulatory benefits – it is the key that opens the door to 80-IAC, angel tax exemption and fast-tracked compliance.

To qualify, the entity must be incorporated as a Private Limited Company, LLP or registered partnership; must be less than 10 years old from the date of incorporation; must have an annual turnover that has not exceeded ₹100 crore in any financial year since incorporation; and must be working towards innovation, development or improvement of products, processes or services, or have a scalable business model with a high potential for employment or wealth creation. The entity cannot be formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business.

Entity form

Pvt Ltd, LLP or registered partnership firm.

Age ceiling

Up to 10 years from the date of incorporation.

Turnover ceiling

Never exceeded ₹100 crore in any year since incorporation.

Innovation test

Innovative / scalable business with clear employment or wealth-creation potential.

Who needs this & why

The benefits every DPIIT-recognised startup enjoys.

savings

80-IAC tax holiday

100% deduction of profits for any 3 consecutive years out of the first 10 years, subject to a separate 80-IAC certificate from the Inter-Ministerial Board.

trending_up

Angel tax exemption

Exemption under section 56(2)(viib) on share premium received from investors – no "excess over fair value" addition in the hands of the startup.

gavel

Self-certification

Self-certify compliance under 6 labour laws and 3 environmental laws for the first 5 years – no inspections, unless a credible complaint is received.

workspace_premium

Fast-track patents

Fast-track examination of patent applications, and concession on statutory filing fees for patents, trademarks and designs through the IP facilitation programme.

task_alt

Public procurement

Exemption from prior turnover and experience criteria in government tenders, listing priority on GeM and EMD waivers.

logout

Easier winding up

Fast-tracked insolvency under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code – recognised startups can wind up within 90 days of filing, if they qualify.

Frequently asked

Startup India questions, real answers.

Is Startup India registration the same as DPIIT recognition?

add

No. Creating a profile on the Startup India portal is a light-touch listing open to almost anyone and does not carry tax benefits. DPIIT recognition is a separate and more rigorous application evaluated against eligibility and innovation criteria, and it is the DPIIT Certificate of Recognition that unlocks the benefits. The profile is the doorway; the recognition is the room.

Does DPIIT recognition automatically give me the 80-IAC tax holiday?

add

No – they are two separate applications. DPIIT recognition qualifies you to apply for 80-IAC, but the tax exemption itself is granted by a separate Inter-Ministerial Board after evaluating the specific innovation and scalability of the business. Only Private Limited Companies and LLPs incorporated within the cut-off date specified in section 80-IAC can apply. The holiday, once approved, is a 100% profit deduction for any three consecutive years out of the first ten.

How does the angel tax exemption work?

add

Under section 56(2)(viib), if an unlisted company issues shares at a premium above the fair market value, the excess is taxed as income from other sources. DPIIT-recognised startups can file a declaration in Form 2 to be exempt from this provision, subject to conditions – including that the aggregate paid-up share capital and share premium after issue does not exceed ₹25 crore, and that investments from certain asset classes (buildings, land, shares, vehicles etc.) are not made for a specified period. We prepare the Form 2 filing and the internal certifications needed.

Can a sole proprietorship apply for DPIIT recognition?

add

No. Only Private Limited Companies, LLPs and registered partnership firms are eligible. Sole proprietorships, HUFs and Section 8 companies (non-profit) are outside the scope. If you are currently a proprietorship with a great startup idea, the practical first step is to incorporate an eligible entity and transition the business into it, after which the DPIIT application can be filed in the new entity's name.

What happens when I cross the turnover or age ceiling?

add

If the entity crosses ₹100 crore turnover in any year, or completes 10 years from the date of incorporation, it ceases to be a "startup" for Startup India purposes. Recognition automatically expires and future benefits stop accruing – however, benefits already availed are not reversed. For founders nearing the cut-off, we plan the timing of 80-IAC and angel-tax claims carefully so that the maximum benefit is captured within the eligibility window.
