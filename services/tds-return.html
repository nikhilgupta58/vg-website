What it is

Deduct, deposit, report – every quarter.

Tax Deducted at Source is the mechanism by which the government collects income tax at the point of payment. Every payer covered under the TDS provisions – salary, contract, professional fee, rent, interest, commission, purchase of immovable property, payments to non-residents – has to deduct tax at the prescribed rate, deposit it with the government and file a quarterly return reporting the deduction.

Deposits are due by the 7th of the month following the month of deduction (30 April for March deductions). Quarterly returns are due by 31 July, 31 October, 31 January and 31 May for the four quarters respectively. Filing is done in TDS return software, validated through the File Validation Utility (FVU) and uploaded on the e-filing portal. Form 16 for salary and Form 16A for non-salary deductions are then generated from TRACES and issued to the deductees.

24Q – Salary

Section 192 salary TDS; Q4 also includes annexure II with full salary break-up.

26Q – Non-salary (resident)

Sections 194A, 194C, 194H, 194I, 194J and most resident payments.

27Q – Non-residents

Payments to non-residents under section 195 – DTAA analysis is critical here.

27EQ – TCS

Tax Collected at Source – scrap sale, liquor, foreign remittance, overseas tours.

Our process

Six disciplined steps, every quarter.

1

Payment review & section mapping

We pull the books, tag each payment to the correct TDS section (192, 194A, 194C, 194I, 194J, 195, etc.) and calculate the deduction at the prescribed rate.

2

Monthly deposit & challans

We ensure the TDS is deposited by the 7th of the following month (30 April for March) via challan ITNS-281 and store the challan details for the quarterly return.

3

Deductee PAN validation

Before preparing the return we validate every deductee PAN against the Income Tax portal to avoid the dreaded short-deduction notice for higher rate under section 206AA / 206AB.

4

Return preparation & FVU

We prepare the correct statement (24Q / 26Q / 27Q / 27EQ), validate it through the latest File Validation Utility and upload it on the e-filing portal within the quarterly due date.

5

Form 16 / 16A download

Once the return is processed on TRACES, we download Form 16 (salary) and Form 16A (non-salary) and share them with the deductees within the statutory window.

6

Defaults & corrections

If TRACES throws up short deduction, short payment, late deduction, late payment or PAN error defaults, we file the correction statement and close the default against the client's conso file.

Frequently asked

TDS questions deductors ask us.

The vendor did not share a PAN – what rate do I deduct?

add

Under section 206AA, if the deductee fails to furnish a valid PAN, the deduction has to be made at the higher of the prescribed rate, the rate in force, or 20%. For section 194-O e-commerce payments it is 5%. Additionally, if the deductee has not filed ITRs as specified under section 206AB, the rate is doubled again. Collecting PAN and doing a quick 206AB search is the cheapest compliance habit a deductor can build.

What happens if I did not deduct TDS at all?

add

Apart from interest at 1% per month from the date TDS should have been deducted until actual deduction, section 40(a)(ia) disallows 30% of the expense while computing business income until the TDS is deducted and paid. That means the expense is effectively added back to profit for the year. Once the TDS is eventually paid, the disallowance can be reversed in the year of payment. There are also penal provisions under section 271C for failure to deduct.

My vendor has a lower deduction certificate. How does that work?

add

Under section 197, a deductee can apply to their Assessing Officer for a lower or nil deduction certificate if the normal TDS rate would result in excess deduction compared to their actual tax liability. The deductee shares this certificate with you along with the certificate number. You then deduct at the reduced rate for payments within the certificate validity period and quote the certificate number in the quarterly return. No certificate, no lower deduction – the certificate is the only legitimate escape hatch.

What is a correction statement and when is it needed?

add

If the original TDS return has an error – wrong PAN, wrong amount, missed challan, wrong section – you file a correction statement on the consolidated file (conso file) downloaded from TRACES. There are different correction types (C1 for deductor details, C2 for challan, C3 for deductee, C4 for salary details, C5 for PAN update, C9 for adding a new challan and linked deductees). Filing corrections promptly avoids default interest and penalty accumulating.

What are the due dates I must remember?

add

Monthly TDS deposit is due on the 7th of the following month, except March deductions which are due 30 April. Quarterly return filing is due 31 July for Q1 (Apr-Jun), 31 October for Q2 (Jul-Sep), 31 January for Q3 (Oct-Dec) and 31 May for Q4 (Jan-Mar). Form 16 for salary must be issued by 15 June and Form 16A within 15 days from the return filing due date. Late filing fee under section 234E is ₹200 per day of delay, capped at the TDS amount.
